Alya Abbott (Apr 21 2022 at 16:34):

What are the ways folks are using the recent topics page? A few I'm aware of are:

  1. Participants in busy communities who don't need to read every topic use it to find ones they are interested in. This one comes up a lot in user interviews.

Alya Abbott (Apr 21 2022 at 16:35):

  1. People who do read everything use it as sort of inbox view (probably with the "Unread" filter?).

Alya Abbott (Apr 21 2022 at 16:36):

  1. I personally use it mostly to check if people have replied to messages I sent recently, i.e. Unread & Participated checked. (cf. #6605)

Because we don't have the "unmute topic" feature yet, it's probably the best way to see if someone responded to you in a muted stream by checking the "Include muted" box in addition to the two above, though I'm not sure that occurred to me until now; I don't think it's easy to discover.

Alya Abbott (Apr 21 2022 at 16:43):

One thing I worry about is that if we make the page look too much like an advanced search page, people will use it as such. At least as currently implemented, this is problematic, because only recent topics are included. But I don't think users necessarily have that "everything older is absent from this page" fact as part of their mental model. It would be hard for them to, as there's no user-comprehensible rule for exactly how much history is there.
