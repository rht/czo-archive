Greg Price (Nov 14 2022 at 20:49):

I see a lot of actions that have two different shortcuts, joined by "or". For many of them, one or both of the shortcuts consists of several keys, joined by "+". In those screenshots, it's a bit confusing to read because there's no visual hierarchy -- in "A + B or C + D", nothing visually conveys that "A + B" is one group and "C + D" is another, vs. "B or C" being grouped together.

Two possible fixes for that:

  • Add more spacing around the "or". Just adding a literal space character might be enough, if it's not there already.
  • Put the alternative on a new line. See the example from Dropbox Paper above in this thread (from 2017).

Greg Price (Nov 15 2022 at 17:42):

In that screenshot from the screen modal, I think the design isn't appropriate for a "tabs" style of switcher. Several problems:

  • When you have tabs, one expects that there's a panel for each tab, and when you select a different tab you see the panel that that tab is connected to. The sort order doesn't behave like this -- it's an option for how to present a single panel, not a selection of different panels -- so tabs aren't a good fit.
  • Similarly, "Subscribed" vs "All streams" isn't two different panels -- it's two filters on a single list. So tabs aren't a good fit.
  • It's weird to have two different sets of "tabs" that are simultaneously controlling a single panel. This is basically another angle on the above two points.
  • In the right half: tabs could be appropriate, as there really are three different panels. But the visual design is off. For example, those blue highlights look like they're trying to be on a dividing line between the tabs and the panel, but the design lacks such a line.
