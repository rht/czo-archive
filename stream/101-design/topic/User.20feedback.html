Sveta Goldstein (May 11 2020 at 10:15):

Hello there #design of Zulip!

An organization I am part of has tried Zulip for week last month. Although we liked Zulip's information architecture from users' perspective quite a lot, sadly we decided not to change our main chat platform just yet.

The most difficult moment of this experiment was our first day, when we (>100ppl) had to confront the unconventionality of the Zulip's UI.
I tried to make it easier for myself and my colleagues and received quite a positive feedback on this custom css here.

There are three main aspects it tackles:

  1. Poor legibility – less dense, more contrasting typography gives more definition to each semantic element: titles, messages, reactions, quotes etc and difference between them

  2. Feeling of being overwhelmed – removed indication of continuity of the stream, hovered messages, greyed out quoted text, use of bg instead of borders to highlight information where possible, other accents help to focus on reading the current message instead of being reminded of the whole context all the time

  3. "Outdated" "look and feel" – rounded corners, thinner borders, typography

I hope you could try it out and consider these points as improvement suggestions very much as I personally miss Zulip's features quite a lot :new_moon_face:
