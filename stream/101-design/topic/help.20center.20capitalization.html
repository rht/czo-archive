Tim Abbott (Dec 01 2021 at 19:40):

Currently we have a few things that are proper nouns:

  • Zulip itself.
  • Pricing plans like Zulip Cloud Standard.
  • Legal documents like the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

"Help Center" is a term that for whatever reason seems to be uniformly treated as a proper noun / capitalized on other sites, in the same way that plan names and legal agreements are. So I'd argue that we should follow that convention and treat "[Zulip] Help Center" as a proper noun too.

Anders Kaseorg (Dec 01 2021 at 19:56):

Terms of Service is capitalized within a legal document because that’s what lawyers do with definitions that they need to refer back to. They’d do the same thing with Compose Box if it was a definition they needed to refer back to. Outside of the document, it need not be capitalized (example).

Anders Kaseorg (Dec 01 2021 at 20:01):

Even inside the legal document, a distinction would be maintained between Terms, referring to a definition made previously referring to the document itself, and terms, referring to other companies’ documents generically (example).

Tim Abbott (Dec 01 2021 at 20:22):

I agree this isn't similar to the legal document case. But it also seems there is a very strong Internet English copywriting convenient that Help Center is a proper noun (enough so that "help center" looks wrong to me when I read it despite having been only using sentence case in my work for many years, and I haven't been able to find a site that doesn't capitalize it when googling). So I think it's worth considering if we want to declare this to be a special case.
