Rishi Gupta (Aug 28 2017 at 17:18):

Continuing a discussion from #5328 here.

I think date changes are more important than timestamps, and you basically always want to know when 3 days passed in between two parts of a conversation, since that almost always has a significant impact on the meaning of the discussion.

I think 90% of the time though, the date change will be the midnight one.
I guess I imagine two different scenarios in which one copies messages:

  • to copy it to someone not in the stream as an fyi (or e.g. someone joined a private stream too late, and didn't get the original messages), where the exact details don't really matter
  • a more legalistic or operational situation where the timing matters a lot

In the first case, I think having it as uncluttered as possible is best.
That being said, I don't feel strongly about this, and it's not a situation that comes up often. If we do keep it, putting it in parens, like Denmark > Denmark1 (Yesterday) instead of Denmark > Denmark1 Yesterday would also help.
