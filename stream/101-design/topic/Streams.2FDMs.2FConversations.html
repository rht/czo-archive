Neil Pilgrim (neiljp) (Feb 04 2023 at 05:02):

Prompted by the discussion in #design > "Private" vs "Direct", I wanted to bring up an observed asymmetry between stream and private/direct messages terminology. While this is somewhat of a documentation issue, it's more regarding pinning down concepts/terms first, which may have impacts on the UI, before updating docs. Maybe to add to a future Zulip glossary?

Examples:

  • "PMs" gets used a lot as an abbreviation in text/discussion; hardly ever a stream equivalent? (SMs?)
  • "PMs and stream messages" feels awkward to keep writing, as would "private- and stream-messages" (or similar for comparisons etc), at least in discussions
  • Other than in a code/API aspect, I second-guess what phrase to use to best convey 'stream messages' (topic? stream-topic?)
  • The UI points out they are PMs or "PRIVATE MESSAGES" (or "DIRECT MESSAGES"), not "PRIVATE" or "DIRECT" (cf "STREAMS")

So, initial questions aimed at improving clarity:

  • terms: should we use 'direct message' and 'stream message', or something clearer for the latter? (do we already define that?)
  • abbreviations: should we avoid abbreviating DMs? Or is there a good equivalent abbreviation for our choice for 'stream message'?
  • left panel: should we title the section 'DIRECT' in place of 'DIRECT MESSAGES'? Or extend 'STREAMS' to be 'STREAM MESSAGES'? (however, see below)

Riffing from that last point and the UI a little further, do we have a written definition of 'conversation'?
I assume, but am unsure if I've seen it defined, as "the set of private messages to the same group of users, or the set of messages to the same topic in a given stream"?
That would suggest that in the left panel, at risk of being too verbose:

  • 'DIRECT MESSAGES' is more accurately 'DIRECT CONVERSATIONS', which would tie into 'more conversations' expanding that list
  • 'STREAMS' may then be a reasonable title to keep, in tandem with expanding 'more topics' to 'more conversation topics'
