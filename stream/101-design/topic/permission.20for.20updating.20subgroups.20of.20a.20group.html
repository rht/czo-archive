Stream: design

Topic: permission for updating subgroups of a group

Sahil Batra (Apr 25 2022 at 12:17):

As Tim Abbott mentions here, we are planning to allow a user to add/remove a subgroup from a user group if the user can see that the subgroup exists, even if user is not a member of that subgroup.

To make it clear, the user needs to have permission to add members to the user group to which subgroups are being added/removed.

Any feedback or thoughts on this?

Alya Abbott (Apr 25 2022 at 16:54):

To the extent that adding people to a group is mostly just giving them permissions, that sounds right.

The one down side of being added to a group that I can think of is that maybe someone would want to get notifications for the inner group but not the outer group. But if it becomes a problem, I think we can fine-tune this by giving users finer-grained control over notifications.

Tim Abbott (Apr 25 2022 at 17:31):

Yeah, that sounds right to me. I suppose the other category that could be problematic is if having another group be a subgroup of a group you manage gives you special permissions to access that group (E.g. if you don't have permission to see its membership, and end up with permission to see it recursive membership).

Tim Abbott (Apr 25 2022 at 17:31):

I expect that sort of thing would be managed when adding that sort of "Access to membership" future restriction in the first place.
