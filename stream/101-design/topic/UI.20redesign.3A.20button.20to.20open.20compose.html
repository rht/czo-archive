Alya Abbott (Jul 20 2022 at 19:45):

Hi folks! Part of Vlad Korobov 's compose box redesign proposal is to have a single button for opening the compose box. The button would have the same default behavior as the long "Message... " we currently have, with the "New stream message" and "New private message" options available via three-dot menu.

I'd like to kick off a conversation about this proposal, as well as the meta question of how we should decide what to do with with aspect of the app.

Alya Abbott (Jul 20 2022 at 19:47):

Both Vlad Korobov and I have the personal experience of never using the "New stream message" and "New private message" buttons, which motivates hiding them behind a menu.

Furthermore, once it's possible switch between stream messages and PMs in the compose box (which is in the works), it will be less crucial to pick the right one to start with.

Thinking about that aspect of it, I guess an alternative proposal would be to keep the long "Message X" button, while still moving "New stream message" and "New private message" behind a menu.

Alya Abbott (Jul 20 2022 at 19:48):

I think there's also significant nervousness around changing the "open compose box" experience, because prior to the current UI, we got feedback from new users that they were confused about how to send a message, and we very much want to avoid that.

Alya Abbott (Jul 20 2022 at 19:58):

In that light, what sort of process would make us feel good about making a major change to the experience of opening the compose box? I could imagine taking some combination of the following steps, and perhaps there are other ideas as well:

  1. Consider a couple of compose box improvements as blockers for making any of the changes discussed above:

    • PM/Stream switcher
    • A button to clear the topic field (labeled "New" in the redesign proposal), which would make "New stream message" less important.

  2. Collect stats on how much "New stream message" and "New private message" get clicked.

  3. Perhaps start by just hiding the "New stream message" and "New private message" buttons, while keeping the long button?
  4. Usability study with brand new users, to minimize the risk of "how do I send a message" confusion.
  5. CZO testing

Tim Abbott (Sep 21 2022 at 19:24):

This seems fairly promising. A few notes on details for the open compose state:

  • I'm not sure about the all-caps labels for STREAMS / DIRECT MESSAGES in that little menu - I feel like it might be better to just style it like other popover menus in the app.
  • I assume it'll still be a lock icon or globe to the left of the stream if the currently selected stream is one of those types; something to keep in mind. I'm also not sure we need the background color around that icon.

The main thing I'm worried about is having Topic: in grey font always visible inside the topic input box. I think it's fine as a placeholder, but I feel like that could end up feeling pretty weird/distracting, especially in any languages where Topic gets translated to something that's like 12 characters. Directionally, I'd rather move towards removing the To: in favor of say, a person icon, over adding Topic: here; once you've sent like 5 messages, you know that box is the topic box, and I think it'd feel to users like clutter. That clutter effect is amplified if we end up with "New topic" inside that input area as well.

One idea for resolving that bundle of issues would be to use the space that you currently have "NEW TOPIC" in for "View topic"; and trying to fit advertising "New topic" as something managed via the typeahead after clicking into that input box, rather than an always visible button. Something I like about having "View topic" in that location is that it can only be visible when you're actually viewing the topic.

That pattern would also let us have the "View conversation" button appear inside the right of the private message recipient area when you're looking at something else might feel consistent an intuitive, replacing "Click on the To button" approach we've been trying, which I can see working but feels a lot less discoverable.

Another benefit of this approach would be that there would be a clear visual signal in the compose box when you're viewing something different from where you're composing. The main downside is that potentially changing what you're viewing with compose to a group PM conversation open would involve the compose box visually changing to line-wrap differently. Though depending what strings we pick, it could be a small effect; "GO TO" would be pretty minimal, vs. something like "View conversation".
