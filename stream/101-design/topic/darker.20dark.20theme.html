Anders Kaseorg said:

I’ve never liked how our dark theme design uses a lighter background with darker boxes on top of it. Most dark themes do the opposite.

Greg Price said:

Anders Kaseorg said:

But an overall redesign of the background colors would be a general improvement if done well.

Yeah, I think the two big reasons it'd be good to have dark mode be darker are:

  • to actually fulfill the point of a dark mode: let the screen emit less light (so it's less disturbing in a dark room, and so it potentially consumes less battery);
  • to have better contrast with text.

Anders Kaseorg (Feb 24 2023 at 18:43):

A dark theme for OLED is a qualitatively different request. An OLED black theme means pure #000000 black, not just darker.

Greg Price (Feb 24 2023 at 18:43):

I previously did a quick survey of some other apps' dark modes.

Our "dark" background is #222d39. Even in the message text input where it's somewhat darker, it's #1b242d.

Gmail's is the lightest I found anywhere else, and it is a lot darker: #111111 generally, and as dark as #040404 in some areas (like for read conversations in the main/conversation-list view.)

GitHub's is #0d1117.

Twitter uses true black, #000.
