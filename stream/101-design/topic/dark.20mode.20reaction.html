Cynthia Lin (Feb 18 2019 at 06:32):

After using dark mode with this message reaction color scheme above for a month, I haven't gotten used to the jarring shade of blue we use for reactions you've reacted to yet. Because of my experience and the fact that there's no other place where that particular shade of blue is used, I'd propose to switch to the following:

  • reactions that you haven't reacted to yet: Transparent background, black outline
  • reaction on hover: Remove the white outline, or change the white outline to match our text color (which isn't pure white). Although I believe it's important to differentiate between hover and not hover, we don't have white outlines when we hover on things like buttons, which I believe reactions should be most similarly styled to resemble. Discord skips the reaction color change as well; the only thing that happens when you hover over a reaction is show who's added a reaction with the emoji you've just hovered over, which we do already.
  • reactions that you've reacted to: Dark black background, black outline, which is the current styling of reactions you haven't added reactions to. I would be open to switching to a shade of blue that fits with the rest of the dark mode color scheme (as in, not unique to message reactions and has been used somewhere else in the codebase).
