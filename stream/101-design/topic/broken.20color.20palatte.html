@Shubham Gupta @Siddharth, you can google for more info, but git bisect is essentially a tool that helps find the specific commit that caused a regression.
It centers on the concept of performing a binary search on the commit list. But instead of manually figuring out which commit to go to next, you just label commits as good or bad, and the bisect automatically shifts you to the next commit, so your task is then just to identify whether you can reproduce on a given commit or not.

Yash RE (Apr 19 2020 at 09:51):

play around with it, you're unlikely to break anything.

Yash RE (Apr 19 2020 at 09:54):

The problem for us, however, is that if you have to run provision between the jumps, it's not worth the time.

Yash RE (Apr 19 2020 at 09:55):

which is why it only really works on a small scale, and doesn't work project wide.
