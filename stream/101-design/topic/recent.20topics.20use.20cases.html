Steve Howell (May 28 2020 at 14:05):

I'm usually not in a huge rush to power through my unreads, so things kind of go in waves:

  • I'll maybe skip 5 topics in a row that don't really interest me
  • I get to a topic where I stop to participate in the discussion.
  • I skip 3 more.
  • I get to a topic where all I want to do is react with a :thumbs_up: and quickly move on
  • I get to a topic where I follow a link.
  • I skip 4 more.
  • I get to a topic that I know concerns me, but it's too much to handle right away, so I star it.
  • I get back to power-through mode and skip or quickly respond to 10
  • you get the idea

Steve Howell (May 28 2020 at 14:06):

What tends to happen for me is that when I finally get to the end of my unreads, I realize that one or two of the topics that I thought were irrelevant have become interesting again.

Steve Howell (May 28 2020 at 14:07):

For example, I might see common trends on three different topics (possibly across three different streams!) that remind me of something that's sort of fundamental.

Steve Howell (May 28 2020 at 14:07):

Or maybe I still don't personally care about the topic, but I want to help somebody else find the more pertinent discussion that I had seen earlier.

Steve Howell (May 28 2020 at 14:08):

So the question is why don't I just do this with the back button (or alt-left)? Well, of course I do that if the topic was only 3 or 4 jumps back. But the back-button approach doesn't really scale. And I want metadata like we have now (in the prototype) to remind me of stuff.
