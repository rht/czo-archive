Alya Abbott (Jan 21 2022 at 22:59):

Hey! What do folks think about making the “Subscribe to more streams” link in the bottom left not sticky?

If you have a small number of subscribed streams (as new users generally do), you would still see it. And if you are subscribed to lots of streams already, you probably aren't adding new streams very often at that point. If you do need to subscribe to another stream and don't think to scroll down, the "+" next to the STREAMS heading will do the job as well.

CC @Vlad Korobov , who proposed the idea. :)

Ken Clary (Jan 21 2022 at 23:01):

I'm not certain the link is needed at all. it's kinda redundant.

Alya Abbott (Jan 21 2022 at 23:06):

I think of it as being primarily helpful for new users. I don't know if @Tim Abbott has any back story on when/why it was added?

Tim Abbott (Jan 22 2022 at 00:56):

Over time we've had a ton of complaints from folks who had trouble figuring out how to subscribe to more streams. The current design of the area next to the STREAMS label is better than the past moment when we added it (I think mainly the + icon is bigger).

Tim Abbott (Jan 22 2022 at 00:58):

I will note that making the element non-sticky could make it substantially harder to notice for anyone joining a large organization that has 20+ default streams. I think this is a pretty important/common situation -- most of our large open communities have a lot of streams.

Tim Abbott (Jan 22 2022 at 00:59):

One detail that may be relevant to understand is that some chat products, most relevantly Discord, don't really have a way to subscribe to a subset of the channels you have access to on a server. As a result, your left sidebar when you join a Discord server has all the channels you can ever access without someone increasing your permissions. I do think it's important to in some way convey that Zulip's left sidebar is not "everything in the server", just "what you are subscribed to", because some large portion of new users will assume incorrectly the wrong answer.
