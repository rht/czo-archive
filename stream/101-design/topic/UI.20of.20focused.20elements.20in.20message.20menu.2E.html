From what I know, the blue box is common for keyboard usage, as it demonstrates where the element of focus is (so that you can use e.g. a screen reader to move to other elements on the page). I agree it can look less nice than no box, but the box is important for accessibility. When using a mouse, you don't need the box because you're clicking on the elements and know where the focus is (you just clicked there) and will use a mouse to click to the next spot (so you don't need to keep track of where focus is on the page).

I'm not an expert on this topic, so someone correct me if this isn't the case, but I believe that's what's going on here :)
