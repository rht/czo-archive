Rohitt Vashishtha (May 01 2017 at 21:49):

Adding to the design principles that @Boris Yankov listed, here's another to potentially consider:

1. A monochromatic or max, trichromatic design with simple geometry(such that it can be expressed as an svg efficiently).
2. A set of well defined color schemes for general use of the logo.

Now, since the logo is mono/di/tri chromatic, it can be printed/used on any media and on any background colors.

SVG(or rather, simple geometric shape) advantage: we can create animated version of the logo (example: transitions in Google Now App).

Limited color advantage: can be made to match the theme of other eleents depending on use (example: Microsoft Windows logo. The one they made for Win 8 onwards). They use it in blue on white background and white on other colors in other backgrounds.

Rohitt Vashishtha (May 01 2017 at 21:50):

Also, 3. Ideally, it might help if it is easy to replicate the logo recognizably by hand in a matter of seconds. Though, this point can be ignored.
