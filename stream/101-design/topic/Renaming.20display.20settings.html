Topic: Renaming display settings

Tim Abbott (May 26 2023 at 18:24):

So the "Display settings" panel in settings is a bit of a misnomer, in that not all of the settings there are accurately described as display settings -- e.g. policies for what to mark as read, or your default view.

image.png

We've sorta been stuck on that name mainly because we don't exactly have something better that's at all specific to describe that bundle of settings.

Tim Abbott (May 26 2023 at 18:25):

The current organization that we have right now is that personal settings are split between 4 panels:

  • Profile
  • Account and privacy
  • Notifications
  • Display settings (everything else).

Tim Abbott (May 26 2023 at 18:25):

(There's a few other panels, but they mostly are tables of things that you can manage, not individual settings).

Tim Abbott (May 26 2023 at 18:27):

There's basically two reasonable things we can do to improve this.

  • One would be to split "Display settings" into a few more coherent categories.
  • And the other would be accept that it's the catch-all for generic personal preference settings and rename it to something like "Preferences", which would I think would feel like the intuitive place to look for "All your user interface configuration settings that aren't your profile, account/security/privacy things, or notifications configuration".

Tim Abbott (May 26 2023 at 18:30):

Thoughts? I think basically renaming it to "Preferences" is probably a good long-term path. The main downside will be that it might grow kinda long/tall over time and feel like something we need to split. If that happens, I think we'd do it in the form of splitting out individual sections into their own pages, and that'd probably feel OK even if those sections are subclasses of preferences.

Karl Stolley (May 26 2023 at 19:01):

I think "Preferences" is superior to "Display settings," and I agree that it also provides better latitude to split things up into additional elements/panes/pages later
