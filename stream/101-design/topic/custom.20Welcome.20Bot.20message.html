Stream: design

Topic: custom Welcome Bot message

Alya Abbott (Apr 25 2022 at 22:38):

Hey! Some organizations want to share introductory content with new members within Zulip, e.g. links to community guidelines, tips about linkifiers or bots, suggestions for how to introduce yourself, etc.

One way we could do this would be allowing orgs to add custom text to the Welcome Bot, e.g. something like:

Hello, and welcome to Zulip!:wave: This is a private message from me, Welcome Bot.

If you are new to Zulip, check out our Getting started guide!

[custom message]

I can also help you get set up! Just click anywhere on this message or press r to reply.

Here are a few messages I understand: apps, profile, theme, streams, topics, message formatting, keyboard shortcuts, help.

Thoughts? There are also some alternative options discussed in an old issue (#3917).

Tim Abbott (Apr 25 2022 at 23:00):

It's a reasonable approach. I worry that extending the welcome bot content has some downsides:

  • The content may not be seen by folks who ignore tutorial-like things.
  • It can't be updated by the community as their policies/etc. change.

But it is an option that would be simpler to implement than a more native feature.

Tim Abbott (Apr 25 2022 at 23:01):

I think Discord is in the process of migrating from an unofficial bot-based approach to having a native community guidelines feature that probably displays in a pop-up when joining the organization or something?

Mario Carneiro (Apr 25 2022 at 23:59):

Hi, I'm a maintainer on the Lean Zulip where this feature came up. I also considered that there could be a security / abuse issue, but it there are already plenty of other official looking places that organizations are allowed to tweak, like the top left icon, the stream headers, linkifiers, and of course anyone with bot permissions can make their own "welcome bot" which would be hard to distinguish from the official one. In short I think the threat model doesn't hold up: organizations already have comparable powers over their own zulip instances.

Mario Carneiro (Apr 26 2022 at 00:02):

The main reason I see to use insertion into a zulip-provided message instead of a complete replacement is if Zulip wants the freedom to change the text if, for example, the list of accepted commands or the keyboard shortcuts changed (since otherwise you would have to ping any organization who had incorporated that text into their organization-specific message so that they can update the message, or let those messages get out of date)

Alya Abbott (Apr 26 2022 at 00:04):

Yeah, I was also thinking that the combo approach is nice to let us update the bits that are about the Zulip app.
