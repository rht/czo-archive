Topic: search default

Alya Abbott (Jul 07 2021 at 21:04):

Hey! When a user does a search, by default we limit it to their history, and offer the following tip when the user scrolls to the top (example search: "foobar"):

End of results from your history. Consider searching all public streams.

Why is that? What are the reasons not to search the entire history by default?

Tim Abbott (Jul 07 2021 at 23:00):

Because the default search searches your PMs and private streams as well, and only searches things you actually received. If you're in only a few streams in a large public organization, the "all public streams" search is going to have 90% of the results be things you never saw, which would make it very hard to find something that you specifically remember seeing.

Alya Abbott (Jul 08 2021 at 00:12):

Hmm, and I guess even searching the full history of the streams you are a member off can interleave things you've never seen if you joined some streams recently...

Neil Pilgrim (neiljp) (Jul 08 2021 at 00:44):

Tim Abbott (Jul 08 2021 at 00:52):

Neil Pilgrim (neiljp) (Jul 08 2021 at 01:09):

That's based on the idea that searches could incrementally be:

  • current subscriptions (+PMs)
  • previous subscriptions +above
  • public-streams +above

That gives a mid-way point which is actually more likely in the "I may have seen this message once" group, whereas public-streams are more of a last-ditch search (or I suppose a more general search for something you may have never seen).
