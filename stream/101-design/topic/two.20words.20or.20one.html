Topic: two words or one

Rishi Gupta (Sep 16 2017 at 16:56):

There are several places we teach users about topics (and we'll probably add a few more over time): hotspots, /help, the second day email we send out, etc. In that advice, should we recommend topics are "2-3 words", or "1-3 words"?

  • The benefit of the first is that I think most one word topics (especially by new folks) are not good topics, and it would have been better if they had been slightly more descriptive. I rarely write one word topics myself.
  • The benefit of the second is that it feels more flexible for new users (they can feel comfortable just using a topic like "hello"), and there are instances where a one word topic is the right thing (e.g. "meeting", "priorities", "introductions").

Thoughts?

Steve Howell (Sep 16 2017 at 17:24):

1-3 is my vote

Greg Price (Sep 23 2017 at 00:31):

One thing that pushes me toward "2-3 words", or even something longer -- note this topic is 4 words :) -- is that a common new-user confusion is to think of "creating" a topic as a heavyweight action, that requires permission (at least social permission). One-word names, like typical stream names, occupy a fat area of namespace, so if you think of topics as likely one word, then that may reinforce a confusion where you think that a new topic means seizing some prime namespace that you'd better not do unless you're really confident. But if you think of topics as typically being several words, then that doesn't fit together with that misunderstanding and you're more likely to shift to the "email subject line" analogy and get the idea better.
